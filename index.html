    Minimizing Costs & Taxes

    How much does your investment portfolio really cost?

    chevron_right Increase Income chevron_left Maximizing Results

    The frank answer for the majority of investors is that they pay a lot more than they think. Most investors know to check the expense ratio on a mutual fund. This is the standard measure of how costly a fund is to own. But that is not the real bottom line.

    There are other costs not reported in the expense ratio, related to buying and selling securities, and these expenses make the cost of owning a mutual fund two or three times more expensive. The main components of hidden costs are: brokerage commissions, bid-ask spreads, taxes due to turnover, size and style cost.
